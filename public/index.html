Welcome to Sacred Heart Parish School

Sacred Heart Parish School serves the Sacred Heart Parish Community by providing a quality Catholic elementary education (K-8) that is based on the Gospel values of Jesus, and is dedicated to the development of the spiritual, intellectual, social-emotional and physical life of our students.

Welcome Note

At Sacred Heart Parish School, we believe in educating the whole child. In our care students will grow intellectually, spiritually, socially, emotionally and physically. We are a small, Catholic, neighborhood school. We recognize each and every student as a child of God with unique talents and gifts. At Sacred Heart, all children have the chance to fulfill their academic and social potential while learning and experiencing the richness of the Catholic faith through developing a relationship with a loving God. Our Catholic faith and values are reflected in every aspect of our school, from the lesson plans to the physical structure. When students enter school each morning, they are greeted by the words, "Jesus, meek and humble of heart, make our hearts like yours." These are more than just reminders; they are the basis of everything we teach.

Spring is here!

Spring is here! Well, almost. As the tule fog lifts we see a springtime that is blooming with activity:

Forms for spring athletics (golf, track, volleyball) are due in the school office by February 28th. Consent forms can be found on Schoolyard, under the “files” tab. Don’t forget the medical form needs to be signed by the doctor. The signed form and the fee for each sport must be received by the office prior to your student beginning practice. Also, consider being a coach for one of our teams. Coaching can be rewarding for you as well as the students.

Our academic decathletes flex their brain power on March 12th as they take on 25 other diocesan schools at the annual Academic Decathlon at Christian Brothers High School. These 12 students have given a tremendous amount of time and effort to memorize super quiz facts about space exploration, Michelangelo, literature, World Youth Day, and early American history. They’ve learned to work and solve logic problems as a team, as well as study for their own individual subject tests.

Also on March 5th, Sacred Heart Parish School hosts the 5th annual crab feed. Socialize from 6 – 7pm, while enjoying appetizers and our No Host Bar (no outside alcohol please). Then it’s an all you can eat, family style meal: clam chowder, salad, pasta w/ meat sauce and the real star of the show- CRAB. If you can still move after all that delicious food, there will be dancing from 9 – midnight. This is an adult evening, so no one under 21 please. Seating is limited; reservations are required.

Mid-March, registration packets for current Sacred Heart School families will be sent home. The completed packets are due back in the office by April 1, 2011, along with a check for registration fees ($275 per student)
