Welcome to Sacred Heart Parish School

Sacred Heart Parish School serves the Sacred Heart Parish Community by providing a quality Catholic elementary education (K-8) that is based on the Gospel values of Jesus, and is dedicated to the development of the spiritual, intellectual, social-emotional and physical life of our students.

Welcome Note

At Sacred Heart Parish School, we believe in educating the whole child. In our care students will grow intellectually, spiritually, socially, emotionally and physically. We are a small, Catholic, neighborhood school. We recognize each and every student as a child of God with unique talents and gifts. At Sacred Heart, all children have the chance to fulfill their academic and social potential while learning and experiencing the richness of the Catholic faith through developing a relationship with a loving God. Our Catholic faith and values are reflected in every aspect of our school, from the lesson plans to the physical structure. When students enter school each morning, they are greeted by the words, "Jesus, meek and humble of heart, make our hearts like yours." These are more than just reminders; they are the basis of everything we teach.

We entered the season of Lent

We entered the season of Lent on February 22nd with a Mass and the mark of ashes on our foreheads to remind us to turn away from sin and return to the gospel. While we are mindful of our human frailties, at Sacred Heart we do not emphasis penance during Lent. Instead, we encourage our students to be proactive, to go out of their way to do something good for someone else. Practicing small acts of kindness during Lent is a great way to "stay in the kindness business" each and every day throughout the year.

REGISTRATION

Registration packets will be going home on March 12th. They must be returned to the school office by March 26. Reminder: children entering kindergarten must be five years old by September 1, 2012 to apply for the 2012/2013 school year.

ACADEMIC DECATHLON

The annual Academic Decathlon will be held on Saturday, March 3rd at Jesuit High School. Thirteen dedicated 7th and 8th graders have been preparing for this event since October. They will be competing against students from 27 other schools in the Logic Quiz, Super Quiz and individual subject testing. This year the subjects for Super Quiz are: The Gospel of John, cell biology, American Impressionism, the Middle Ages, and the literature selection is Farewell to Manzanar. The decathletes have spent many after-school hours studying for this event. We wish you luck, brainiacs!

CONFIRMATION

Our 7th graders have been diligently preparing for the Sacrament of Confirmation and are now ready to fully commit to their Christian faith as young adults. This rite of passage will be held at Sacred Heart Church on Sunday, March 18, at 1:30pm.

MISSOULA CHILDREN’S THEATRE

Missoula Children’s Theatre rolls into Sacred Heart on March 19th – 23rd. This year’s presentation is "King Arthur’s Quest". There will be two performances on March 24th – a matinee at 3:00pm and an evening show at 7:30pm. The theatre workshop is open to all children grades 1 – 8. Roles in the play will be cast using a lottery system. In order to participate in the theatre camp, students will need to be at all of the rehearsals and available during the week of camp. Parent participation is required.

SPRING SPORTS

Forms for spring athletics (golf, track, boys’ volleyball) are due in the school office by February 28th. Consent forms can be found on Schoolyard, under the “Files” tab. Don’t forget the medical form needs to be signed by the doctor. The signed form and fee for each sport must be received by the office prior to your student beginning practice.

Track and Field is open to students in 5th – 8th grade. There will be a parent meeting for track & field on Wednesday, February 29, at 7:00 in the cafeteria to discuss details.
